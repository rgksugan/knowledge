# The House Of Kanooru
by [[Kuvempu]]

## Highlights
> In that great still world, the cart was the only moving object.

> She was no more a swaying creeper in the cool breeze of a spring morning, but a sturdy banana stem bent under the weight of the fruit, surrounded by saplings, big and small, at its root.

> There were also a couple of portraits of patriots and a picture of Mother India, proof that the influence of the growing nationalist movement had showered its benevolent blessings over even the corners of Malenadu.

> A debt is like a grinding stone around one's neck.

> The areas of his stomach, chest, shoulders and forehead which had always worn namas and known no sun were blanched and looked as if the namas were already painted on.

> Unknown to the Gowda a few insects had made their homes in the crevices very much like the atheists, keeping their views secret in order to survive in a society of believers.

> Having bathed a little while ago and in a freshly washed saree, she looked like a pretty rain-washed lotus bud.

> When he left the hut at dawn, Obayya was no more the boy he had been the previous day. He had come to know of a sweet world of utterly new experience.

> The toddy shop which was silent during the day like a sage in contemplation turned into a fearsome and ugly haunt for the demons of the night.

> The Goddess of Night gently draped her seragu over the forest-clad range of the Sahyadri mountains.

> Their eyes met and it was an indescribable event—a meeting of two pairs of loving eyes, more holy, more mysterious and more significant than the confluence of the Thunga and the Bhadra, of the Ganga and the Yamuna.

> At other times his sharp intelligence would have helped him ignore such events. But he was in love and his captive heart saw nothing but omens everywhere.

> In the flood waters of her love, the entire universe with its countless stars and planets, infinite space and time appeared to be no more than a bubble floating helplessly on the surface.

> Just as people nowadays consider offering coffee and drinking it a sign of modernity and civilized respect, people in those days similarly honoured liquor and toddy.

> The blood-coloured, red-eyed sun was slowly setting, shining bright behind the forests of the Western Ghats. A quarter of it disappeared even as one looked, then half and then it was totally gone. The evening light sprayed the forests and hills orange.

> Their philosophy and experience maintained that it was to be love after marriage and not marriage after love.

> The happy optimist was slowly becoming a pessimist schooled by frustration.

> At that moment his son's disobedience, iniquity and rascality were all wiped away and only the pure love natural to a father emerged.
