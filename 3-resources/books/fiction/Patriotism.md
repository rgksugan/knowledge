# Patriotism
by [[Yukio Mishima]]

## Highlights
> Neither spoke the thought, but their hearts, their bodies, and their pounding breasts blazed with the knowledge that this was the very last time.

> It was as if the words “The Last Time” were spelled out, in invisible brushstrokes, across every inch of their bodies.

> Wherever the lieutenant’s eyes moved his lips faithfully followed.

> It was as if something which her modesty had wished to keep hidden to the end had suddenly burst its bonds of constraint.

> They remembered the taste of kisses which had never wearied, the touch of naked flesh, episode after episode of dizzying bliss.

> Death of any sort is a fearful thing to watch.

> It was the first time Reiko had ever seen her husband’s blood and she felt a violent throbbing in her chest.

> Ever since her marriage her husband’s existence had been her own existence, and every breath of his had been a breath drawn by herself.
